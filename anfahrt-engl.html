Directions

On a hill between fruit orchards and mixed forests between Saalfeld and Rudolstadt you can find the small village of Schloßkulm. The German term "Schloß" means castle - nowadays you will not find any castle no longer but 14 half-timbered houses and a central baking house still. About 50 inhabitants are living and bringing life to the village nowadays as well as farming in the areas around it. It is easy to reach the cities Erfurt, Jena, Weimar and the Thuringian forest that will lead you through dreamlike landscapes.

Means of Transport

If you would like to come by bike you will need some stamina, patience and trained legs. Public transport is reduced to a school bus line and not recommended.

Groceries

There are no shops or supermarkets in the village! You can pick up eggs in the village on request. The closest supermarkets are located in Rudolstadt (eco store amongst others)/Saalfeld ( teegut)/Etzelbach (farm shop).
